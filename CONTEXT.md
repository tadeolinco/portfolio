## Employment and career arc

**Employment entry**:
A company block on the resume: organization name, one or more dated roles, and achievement bullets.
_Avoid_: Job, position, work history row

**Role**:
A job title held during a specific period at one company.
_Avoid_: Position, title line

**Short stint**:
An employment entry short enough that the resume needs honest context (roughly under four months).
_Avoid_: Gap, job hop, red flag

**Restructuring exit**:
Departure or survival context when an employer cut headcount at scale (~70% at OOZOU) due to financial distress—not a performance narrative.
_Avoid_: Laid off (without context), fired, let go

## Resume presentation

**Focus line**:
The positioning sentence under the resume name and title; names domain and strengths, not generic traits alone.
_Avoid_: Summary, objective, headline

**Skills section**:
Listed capabilities and toolchain on the resume (e.g. React, Playwright)—not repositories or accounts mistaken for skills.
_Avoid_: Tech stack, technologies

**Bullet**:
One achievement line under an employment entry; states outcome, scope, or metric.
_Avoid_: Responsibility, duty, bullet point

**Vague bullet**:
A bullet that names activity without outcome (e.g. "introduced best practices"); unacceptable for Pomelo Pay and weak entries.
_Avoid_: Soft-skill line, filler

**Side project**:
A shipped personal product on the resume, distinct from paid employment.
_Avoid_: Hobby, weekend project (when describing Can Book–level work)

**Flagship side project**:
Can Book—the letterboxd-style cinema showtimes product (canbook.sh); leads the side projects section.
_Avoid_: Main project, personal app (too generic)
